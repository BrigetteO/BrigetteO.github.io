Cold Brew

A summertime favorite

I distinctly remember my first cold brew coffee (with coconut milk, of course). I was sitting in the sun at Pannikin after a long run with friends. Nothing had ever tasted so delicious.


How it's made

  1. Grind 3/4 cup of beans very coarsely per 4 cups of water
  2. Soak the coarse-ground beans in room temperature (or cold) water for 12 hours or more
  3. Filter the grounds out of the water using a paper coffee filter, fine metal sieve, French press etc.

Exciting enhancements

Cold brew + Nitro!

A sweeter and creamier version of the original can be made by adding nitrogen, which is typically stored in a pressurized keg and poured from a tap.
